Arrays


Syntax:

	<data-type> <variable-name>[ <size>  ]; 

Example:
	
	int my_awesome_array[69];

This creates an array of 69 integers.

Think of arrays as how a group of related numbers are written in maths:
 n1, n2, n3, ... 

The numbers at the bottom of each n is called the subscript.
So, n1 is the 1st n, n2 is the 2nd n etc.

In computers, the subscripts are called indexes. And they start from 0,
and not from 1, as is the convention in maths.

What you'd write as n2 in maths, would be written as n[2]
(being careful about the 0-based index).
